An Analysis on the prediction of car crashes In todays world, most of us of age 18 and older have cars. But with such great responsibility comes great risks. Car crashes are very prominent in today's day and age with lots of factors coming into play. Some of those factors include Weather, Time of the day driving, Driver Experience, Road Condition and many more. My project contains a dataset from https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/denkuznetz/traffic-accident-prediction which will help me analyze and predict the chances of a car crash based on circumstances.

Analyzing brings me to ask the following questions:

  • How does traffic density affect accidents?
  • Does weather affect Accident rates?
  • Does alcohol play a role in the rate of crashes?
  • Is it safer to drive in Daylight or Nightime?
  • Does road condition play a role in Car crashes?
  • Does road type influence crashes ?

QUESTION 1: HOW DOES TRAFFIC DENSITY AFFECTS ACCIDENTS?

In[7]:
data = pd.read_csv('dataset_traffic_accident_prediction1.csv')

# Grouping data by Traffic Density and calculating average accidents
traffic_density_accidents = data.groupby('Traffic_Density')['Accident'].mean().reset_index()

# Plotting the results
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
plt.bar(traffic_density_accidents['Traffic_Density'], traffic_density_accidents['Accident'], width=0.5)
plt.xlabel('Traffic Density')
plt.ylabel('Average Number of Accidents')
plt.title('Impact of Traffic Density on Accidents')
plt.xticks(traffic_density_accidents['Traffic_Density'])
plt.grid(axis='y', linestyle='--', alpha=0.7)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Based on my analysis and Plotting, Traffic density does not seem to play an important role in the causation of accidents. Which if you think about it, makes sense because when there is more traffic, people tend to be more alert reducing the amount of crashes.

Q2:Does weather affect Accident rates?

In[8]:
data = pd.read_csv('dataset_traffic_accident_prediction1.csv')
weather_accidents = data.groupby('Weather')['Accident'].mean().reset_index()

# Plotting the results
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
plt.bar(weather_accidents['Weather'], weather_accidents['Accident'], color='orange', width=0.5)
plt.xlabel('Weather Conditions')
plt.ylabel('Average Number of Accidents')
plt.title('Impact of Weather on Accidents')
plt.xticks(rotation=45, ha='right')
plt.grid(axis='y', linestyle='--', alpha=0.7)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Analysis :Clear weather has a high rate of Accidents and this leads me to believe it is because most drivers are overconfident when driving in Pleasant weather. However, storms have the highest average number of crashes and this leads me to believe it is because unexpected things happen and stormy weather, the roads are very slick and slippery visibility is low and so much more stuff which is unexpected. Raininess is the lowest and this leads me to believe it is because most drivers are cautious when it starts to rain. So in conclusion I believe weather definitely plays A Part in the amount of crashes, but it all boils down to how alert the driver is.
